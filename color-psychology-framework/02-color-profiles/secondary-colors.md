# Secondary Colors: Green, Orange, Purple

## Green (#00FF00)

### Psychology & Meaning
Green is the most restful color for the human eye. It symbolizes nature, growth, and renewal. Green has therapeutic effects, reducing stress and promoting balance.

### Associations
| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| Growth, renewal, vitality | Envy, jealousy |
| Health, wellness, nature | Inexperience ("green") |
| Balance, harmony, stability | Stagnation, boredom |
| Wealth, prosperity | Possessiveness |

### Best Uses in Branding
- **Eco/Sustainability** — Environmental focus (Whole Foods, BP, Animal Planet)
- **Health & Wellness** — Natural, organic (Tropicana, Spotify)
- **Finance** — Money, growth (TD Bank, Mint, Robinhood)
- **Outdoor/Nature** — Adventure, environment (John Deere, Land Rover)

### When to Avoid
- Luxury brands seeking exclusivity
- High-energy, excitement-driven brands
- Technology (unless eco-focused)
- Fast-paced, urgent contexts

### Shades & Their Uses
| Shade | Hex | Best For |
|-------|-----|----------|
| Bright Green | #00FF00 | Energy, digital, innovation |
| Forest Green | #228B22 | Nature, organic, stability |
| Lime | #32CD32 | Youthful, energetic, fresh |
| Olive | #808000 | Military, outdoor, earthy |
| Mint | #98FF98 | Fresh, calm, feminine |

## Orange (#FFA500)

### Psychology & Meaning
Orange combines the energy of red with the cheerfulness of yellow. It is warm, enthusiastic, and stimulating. Orange promotes creativity, adventure, and social interaction.

### Associations
| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| Enthusiasm, energy, warmth | Immaturity, frivolity |
| Creativity, adventure | Cheapness (if overused) |
| Friendliness, confidence | Overwhelming, loud |
| Fun, playfulness | Lack of sophistication |

### Best Uses in Branding
- **Food & Beverage** — Appetite and warmth (Fanta, Dunkin')
- **Sports & Fitness** — Energy and action (Gatorade, Nike accents)
- **Youth Brands** — Fun and playful (Nickelodeon, Soundcloud)
- **Call-to-Actions** — Stands out without aggression

### When to Avoid
- Luxury or premium brands
- Corporate or conservative industries
- Healthcare (except for energy/vitality products)
- Brands seeking to appear traditional or serious

### Shades & Their Uses
| Shade | Hex | Best For |
|-------|-----|----------|
| Bright Orange | #FFA500 | CTAs, energy, youthful |
| Tangerine | #FF9966 | Friendly, approachable |
| Burnt Orange | #CC5500 | Autumn, earthy, sophisticated |
| Peach | #FFCBA4 | Soft, feminine, gentle |
| Coral | #FF7F50 | Warm, modern, trendy |

## Purple (#800080)

### Psychology & Meaning
Purple combines the stability of blue with the energy of red. Historically associated with royalty due to the rarity of purple dyes, it conveys luxury, creativity, and wisdom.

### Associations
| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| Luxury, royalty, elegance | Arrogance, excess |
| Creativity, imagination | Moodiness, immaturity |
| Wisdom, spirituality | Introversion, mystery |
| Mystery, magic, uniqueness | Decadence |

### Best Uses in Branding
- **Luxury & Premium** — Exclusivity (Cadbury, Hallmark)
- **Beauty & Cosmetics** — Femininity, elegance (Urban Decay)
- **Creative Industries** — Innovation, imagination (Twitch, Roku)
- **Spiritual/Wellness** — Wisdom, mindfulness (meditation apps)

### When to Avoid
- Budget or value-focused brands
- Masculine-targeted products (in traditional markets)
- Agricultural or outdoor brands
- Conservative corporate environments

### Shades & Their Uses

|-------|-----|----------|

| Lavender | #E6E6FA | Calm, feminine, gentle |
| Violet | #8F00FF | Creative, bold, unique |
| Plum | #DDA0DD | Sophisticated, muted, elegant |
| Magenta | #FF00FF | Bold, modern, attention-grabbing |

## LLM-ACTIONABLE-INSIGHTS

### Quick Decision Guide for Secondary Colors

**Choose GREEN when:**
- Brand focuses on health, wellness, or sustainability
- Need to convey growth, balance, or freshness
- Industry is organic, environmental, or financial
- Goal is to create calm, trustworthy atmosphere

**Choose ORANGE when:**
- Brand is playful, energetic, or youthful
- Need effective call-to-action colors
- Target audience values fun and creativity
- Industry is food, entertainment, or sports

**Choose PURPLE when:**
- Brand wants to convey luxury or premium quality
- Target audience values creativity or spirituality
- Industry is beauty, creative arts, or wellness
- Goal is to stand out as unique and imaginative

### Secondary Color Combinations
- **Green + Brown** — Organic, natural, earthy
- **Green + Blue** — Fresh, environmental, tech-forward
- **Orange + Blue** — Dynamic contrast, engaging
- **Orange + Black** — Bold, Halloween, energetic
- **Purple + Gold** — Luxury, premium, royal
- **Purple + Silver** — Modern, tech, sophisticated
