## Detailed Industry Breakdowns

### Technology & Software

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Blue (trust, innovation)
- **Secondary:** White, Gray (clean, modern)
- **Accent:** Green, Orange (for CTAs)

**Why Blue Dominates:**
- Conveys trust and reliability
- Associated with intelligence and innovation
- Calming for users spending long hours with products

**Differentiation Options:**
- Purple for creative/AI focus
- Green for eco-conscious tech
- Orange for consumer-focused, playful products

**Examples:** Facebook (blue), Spotify (green), Slack (purple)

---

### Finance & Banking

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Blue (trust), Green (money, growth)
- **Secondary:** White, Gray (professionalism)
- **Accent:** Gold (premium services)

**Color Psychology in Finance:**
- Blue signals security and dependability
- Green connects to money and growth
- Avoid red (associated with losses)

**Traditional vs. Modern:**
- Traditional: Navy, gold, conservative palette
- Modern/Fintech: Brighter blues, gradients, more color

**Examples:** Chase (blue), TD Bank (green), Robinhood (green)

---

### Healthcare & Pharmaceuticals

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Blue (calm, trust), Green (health)
- **Secondary:** White (cleanliness)
- **Accent:** Light blue, soft green

**Color Psychology in Healthcare:**
- Blue calms anxious patients
- Green symbolizes health and healing
- White conveys cleanliness and sterility

**Considerations:**
- Avoid red (blood, emergency)
- Warmer tones for wellness/alternative medicine
- Bright colors for children's healthcare

**Examples:** Pfizer (blue), CVS (red — pharmacy exception), Johnson & Johnson (red — legacy brand)

---

### Food & Beverage

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Red, Orange, Yellow (appetite stimulants)
- **Secondary:** Green (healthy options), Brown (coffee/chocolate)
- **Accent:** White (clean), Black (premium)

**Color Psychology in Food:**
- Red and yellow stimulate appetite
- Green signals healthy, fresh, organic
- Blue suppresses appetite (rarely used)

**Category Variations:**
- Fast food: Red, yellow, orange
- Healthy/Organic: Green, brown, earth tones
- Premium dining: Black, gold, burgundy
- Coffee: Brown, warm tones

**Examples:** McDonald's (red/yellow), Starbucks (green), Häagen-Dazs (burgundy)

---

### Luxury & Premium

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Black (sophistication), Gold (prestige)
- **Secondary:** White (purity), Deep purple
- **Accent:** Metallic finishes

**Color Psychology in Luxury:**
- Black signals exclusivity and timelessness
- Gold represents wealth and achievement
- Purple historically linked to royalty

**Design Principles:**
- Minimalism and restraint
- High contrast for impact
- Quality in execution matters more than color

**Examples:** Chanel (black/white), Rolex (gold/green), Gucci (earth tones)

---

### Children's Products

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Bright primaries (red, blue, yellow)
- **Secondary:** Green, orange, purple
- **Full spectrum approach common**

**Color Psychology for Children:**
- Bright colors attract attention
- Primary colors feel safe and familiar
- Variety stimulates creativity

**Age Considerations:**
- Toddlers: Bright, simple primaries
- Older kids: More sophisticated palettes
- Tweens: Trend-influenced choices

**Examples:** LEGO (bright primaries), Nickelodeon (orange), Disney (varied)

---

### Fitness & Sports

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Red, Orange, Black
- **Secondary:** Blue (endurance), Green (outdoor)
- **Accent:** Neon/bright for energy

**Color Psychology in Fitness:**
- Red increases heart rate and energy
- Orange encourages activity and enthusiasm
- Black conveys power and determination

**Category Variations:**
- Gym/CrossFit: Red, black, aggressive
- Yoga/Mindfulness: Purple, blue, calm
- Outdoor/Adventure: Green, earth tones

**Examples:** Nike (black/red), Under Armour (black), Lululemon (red)

---

### Beauty & Cosmetics

**Recommended Colors:**
- **Primary:** Pink, Purple, Black
- **Secondary:** Gold, Rose gold, White
- **Varies by positioning**

**Color Psychology in Beauty:**
- Pink and purple feel feminine and luxurious
- Black signals high-end sophistication
- Natural brands use earth tones

**Positioning Through Color:**
- Mass market: Brighter, more playful
- Premium: Black, gold, sophisticated
- Natural/Organic: Green, brown, neutral

**Examples:** Sephora (black/white), MAC (black), Glossier (millennial pink)

## LLM-ACTIONABLE-INSIGHTS

### Industry Color Selection Process

1. **Identify the industry category**
2. **Determine positioning** (budget, mid-market, premium)
3. **Check competitor colors**
4. **Select based on industry norms OR differentiate strategically**
5. **Validate against target audience**

### Quick Decision Framework

```
IF industry is traditional (finance, law, healthcare):
    → Lean toward blue, conservative palettes

IF industry is creative (design, entertainment, tech startup):
    → More flexibility, consider differentiation

IF industry is food/beverage:
    → Warm colors, appetite-stimulating palette

IF industry is luxury:
    → Black, gold, deep sophisticated tones

IF industry targets children:
    → Bright, playful, primary colors
```

### Questions to Ask:
- "What industry are you in?"
- "Are you positioning as traditional or disruptive?"
- "Premium, mid-market, or budget positioning?"
- "What do your competitors' color palettes look like?"
