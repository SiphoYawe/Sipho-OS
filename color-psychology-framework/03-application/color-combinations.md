# Color Combinations Guide

## Color Harmony Fundamentals

### Complementary Colors
Colors opposite each other on the color wheel.
- **High contrast, vibrant**
- **Best for:** Making elements stand out

| Combination | Effect | Use Case |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Blue + Orange | Dynamic, energetic | Sports, creative brands |
| Red + Green | Bold, festive | Seasonal, but use carefully |
| Yellow + Purple | Striking, creative | Arts, children's brands |

### Analogous Colors
Colors adjacent on the color wheel.
- **Harmonious, natural**
- **Best for:** Cohesive, unified designs

| Combination | Effect | Use Case |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Blue + Green + Teal | Calm, natural | Wellness, environmental |
| Red + Orange + Yellow | Warm, energetic | Food, entertainment |
| Purple + Blue + Violet | Creative, luxurious | Beauty, creative industries |

### Triadic Colors
Three colors equally spaced on the wheel.
- **Balanced, vibrant**
- **Best for:** Dynamic, varied palettes

| Combination | Effect | Use Case |
|-------------|--------|----------|
| Red + Yellow + Blue | Primary, bold | Children's products |
| Orange + Green + Purple | Secondary, creative | Arts, unique brands |
| Teal + Orange + Magenta | Modern, bold | Digital, tech startups |

### Split-Complementary
One color plus the two adjacent to its complement.
- **Contrast with less tension**
- **Best for:** Sophisticated contrast

## Color Pairing Do's and Don'ts

### DO:

| Practice | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Use the 60-30-10 rule | 60% primary, 30% secondary, 10% accent |
| Ensure sufficient contrast | Dark text on light backgrounds (or vice versa) |
| Test for accessibility | Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for text |
| Consider context | Colors behave differently across mediums |
| Limit your palette | 3-5 colors maximum |

### DON'T:

| Mistake | Why It's Problematic |
|---------|---------------------|
| Use too many bright colors together | Overwhelming, amateur look |
| Pair similar saturations without contrast | Elements blend together |
| Ignore cultural associations | Can offend or confuse |
| Use red and green for critical info | Color blindness accessibility |
| Choose based only on trends | May date quickly |

## Context-Specific Combinations

### Digital/Web Design

**Primary Button (CTA):**
- Orange on white (high visibility)
- Blue on white (trustworthy)
- Green on white (positive action)

**Background Combinations:**
- White + Dark text (classic readability)
- Light gray + Dark text (softer)
- Dark mode: Dark gray + Light text

**Avoid:**
- Blue links on blue backgrounds
- Red text on green (or vice versa)
- Low contrast combinations

### Print Materials

**Business Cards:**
- Black + White + Accent color
- Navy + Gold
- Brand colors with high contrast

**Brochures/Marketing:**
- Hero color (60%) + Supporting (30%) + Accent (10%)
- White space is crucial
- Consider paper color interaction

### Social Media

**High Engagement Colors:**
- Bright, saturated colors perform better
- Blue and orange drive engagement
- Consistent brand colors build recognition

## LLM-ACTIONABLE-INSIGHTS

### Color Combination Decision Tree

```
What is the primary goal?

├── Build Trust
│   └── Blue + White/Gray
│
├── Create Excitement
│   └── Red/Orange + Black/White
│
├── Convey Luxury
│   └── Black + Gold/Purple
│
├── Show Innovation
│   └── Blue + Orange or Purple + Teal
│
├── Express Nature/Health
│   └── Green + Brown/White
│
└── Be Friendly/Approachable
    └── Orange/Yellow + Blue/White
```

### Questions to Determine Combinations:
1. "What is the primary emotion you want to evoke?"
2. "What is your secondary message?"
3. "What colors are you drawn to or want to avoid?"
4. "Where will these colors primarily appear? (digital, print, products)"

### Validation Checklist:
- [ ] Colors align with brand personality
- [ ] Sufficient contrast for accessibility
- [ ] Works in both digital and print
- [ ] Differentiates from competitors
- [ ] Appropriate for target audience
