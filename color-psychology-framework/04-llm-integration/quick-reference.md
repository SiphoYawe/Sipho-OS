# Color Psychology Quick Reference

## One-Page Color Guide

### Primary Colors at a Glance

| Color | Emotion | Best For | Avoid |
|-------|---------|----------|-------|
| **Red** | Passion, urgency, energy | Food, sales, entertainment | Healthcare, finance |
| **Blue** | Trust, calm, professionalism | Tech, finance, healthcare | Food, urgency |
| **Yellow** | Optimism, creativity, attention | Children's, creative | Luxury, serious contexts |

### Secondary Colors at a Glance

| Color | Emotion | Best For | Avoid |
|-------|---------|----------|-------|
| **Green** | Growth, health, nature | Eco, wellness, finance | High-energy brands |
| **Orange** | Enthusiasm, warmth, fun | Youth, CTAs, food | Luxury, corporate |
| **Purple** | Luxury, creativity, wisdom | Premium, beauty, creative | Budget brands |

### Neutral Colors at a Glance

| Color | Emotion | Best For | Avoid |
|-------|---------|----------|-------|
| **Black** | Sophistication, power | Luxury, fashion, tech | Children's, healthcare |
| **White** | Purity, simplicity, clean | Minimalist, healthcare, tech | Warmth-seeking brands |
| **Gray** | Balance, professionalism | Corporate, tech | Playful, energetic brands |
| **Brown** | Reliability, earthiness | Organic, outdoor | Modern tech |

## The 60-30-10 Rule

```
60% — PRIMARY COLOR
      Dominant color, backgrounds, main brand areas

30% — SECONDARY COLOR
      Supporting color, sections, navigation

10% — ACCENT COLOR
      CTAs, highlights, attention-grabbing elements
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Too many colors | Limit to 3-5 max |
| Low contrast | Ensure 4.5:1 ratio minimum |
| Personal preference over strategy | Use research and psychology |
| Ignoring industry norms | Research competitors first |
| Forgetting accessibility | Test for color blindness |
| Inconsistent usage | Create and follow brand guide |

---

## Accessibility Quick Check

### Minimum Contrast Ratios (WCAG)
- **Normal text:** 4.5:1
- **Large text:** 3:1
- **UI components:** 3:1

### Color Blindness Considerations
- Don't rely on color alone for information
- Avoid red/green combinations for critical info
- Use patterns/shapes in addition to color
- Test with color blindness simulators

## Quick Decision Framework

```
STEP 1: What emotion? → Narrows to 2-3 colors
STEP 2: What industry? → Validates or adjusts
STEP 3: Who's audience? → Refines shade/tone
STEP 4: Competition check → Differentiation?
STEP 5: Apply 60-30-10 → Build palette
```

---

## Color Psychology Formulas

### Brand Palette Formula
```
Brand Color = (Core Emotion) + (Industry Fit) + (Audience Appeal) + (Differentiation)
```

### Selection Criteria Checklist
```
□ Aligns with brand personality
□ Appropriate for industry
□ Appeals to target audience
□ Differentiates from competitors
□ Works across applications
□ Meets accessibility standards
□ Has longevity (not just trending)
```
