# Color Psychology Prompt Templates

## Brand Color Selection Session

### Opening Prompt
```
I'll help you select the perfect colors for your brand. To give you the best recommendations, I need to understand a few things:

1. **Industry:** What industry or sector is your brand in?
2. **Brand Personality:** Describe your brand in 3-5 words (e.g., innovative, trustworthy, playful)
3. **Target Audience:** Who is your ideal customer? (age, demographics, preferences)
4. **Competitors:** Who are your top 2-3 competitors?
5. **Emotion:** What feeling should people have when they see your brand?

Take your time with these questions—they'll guide us to the right colors.
```

### Follow-up Probes
```
That's helpful. A few more questions to refine the recommendation:

- Do you want to BLEND IN with industry norms or STAND OUT?
- Are there any colors you're drawn to or want to avoid?
- Where will these colors primarily appear? (website, packaging, signage)
- Do you have any existing brand elements we need to consider?
```

### Recommendation Delivery Template
```
Based on what you've shared, here's my color recommendation:

## Your Brand Color Palette

**PRIMARY COLOR: [Color Name] ([Hex Code])**
→ Why: [1-2 sentences explaining fit with brand personality and industry]
→ Use for: Main brand elements, backgrounds, headers

**SECONDARY COLOR: [Color Name] ([Hex Code])**
→ Why: [1-2 sentences on how it complements primary]
→ Use for: Supporting elements, sections, navigation

**ACCENT COLOR: [Color Name] ([Hex Code])**
→ Why: [1-2 sentences on the energy/contrast it adds]
→ Use for: CTAs, highlights, key actions (use sparingly—10% of palette)

## Palette in Action
- [Example of how colors work together]
- [Brands with similar successful palettes]

## Next Steps
1. Test these colors on your website/materials mockups
2. Check contrast ratios for accessibility
3. Validate with your target audience if possible

Does this palette feel right for your brand? Any adjustments needed?
```

## Industry-Specific Consultation

### Tech/SaaS Brand
```
For a tech brand like yours, color psychology suggests:

**Trust is paramount** → Blue is your friend (used by IBM, Facebook, LinkedIn)
**Innovation signal** → Consider purple accents (used by Twitch, Roku)
**Clean/Modern** → White space and minimal palette

**Recommendation for [Brand Name]:**
Primary: [Blue shade] for trust and professionalism
Secondary: [Light gray/white] for clean, modern feel
Accent: [Bright accent] for CTAs and energy

**Warning signs to avoid:**
- Too many colors (looks amateur)
- Dark/heavy palette (can feel outdated)
- Ignoring accessibility (tech users expect it)
```

### Food/Beverage Brand
```
For food and beverage brands, color psychology is crucial:

**Appetite stimulants:** Red, Orange, Yellow increase hunger
**Health signals:** Green, Brown, Earth tones = natural/organic
**Premium signals:** Black, Gold, Burgundy = high-end

**For your [product type]:**
Primary: [Color] to [stimulate appetite/signal health/convey premium]
Secondary: [Color] to [support the feeling]
Accent: [Color] for [packaging pop/CTA/highlight]

**Brands doing this well:** [Examples]
**What to avoid:** Blue (suppresses appetite, unless you're a water brand)
```

### Healthcare/Wellness Brand
```
Healthcare brands need to balance trust with warmth:

**Clinical trust:** Blue + White = clean, professional
**Warmth/Care:** Add green or soft accent = approachable
**Avoid:** Red (blood/emergency), Dark colors (anxiety)

**For your [service type]:**
Primary: [Blue/Green shade] for calm and trust
Secondary: [Soft neutral] for approachability
Accent: [Warm touch] to humanize

**Balance:** You want to feel professional but not cold
```

## Problem-Solving Prompts

### "My colors aren't working"
```
Let's diagnose what might be off:

**Current Palette:** [Their colors]

Possible issues:
1. **Contrast problem** → Colors too similar in value/saturation
2. **Message mismatch** → Colors don't align with brand personality
3. **Industry disconnect** → Colors send wrong signals for your sector
4. **Overload** → Too many colors competing for attention

**Based on [specific issue identified]:**
Recommendation: [Specific fix with reasoning]
```

### "I can't decide between colors"
```
Let's compare your options:

**Option A: [Color]**
- Message it sends: [perception]
- Best for: [use cases]
- Similar brands: [examples]

**Option B: [Color]**
- Message it sends: [perception]
- Best for: [use cases]
- Similar brands: [examples]

**For your brand specifically:**
I'd lean toward [choice] because [reasoning tied to their brand/audience]

But ultimately, if you feel strongly about the other option, [consideration for that choice]
```

### "What color should my CTA be?"
```
For effective CTAs, you want contrast with your primary palette:

**Your primary color:** [Color]
**Best CTA options:**
1. [Complementary color] — Highest contrast, most attention
2. [Warm accent] — Energetic, action-oriented
3. [Green] — Positive, "go" signal

**My recommendation:** [Color] because [it contrasts well with your palette / conveys action / etc.]

**Proven combinations:**
- Blue site + Orange CTA (classic for a reason)
- White site + Green CTA (clean, positive)
- Dark site + Bright accent CTA (high visibility)
```
